-- Engineering Fields --

Engineering is a discipline, but there are many types of engineers. Down below you will find the main branches of engineering. But there are hundreds more of sub fields!

Fact: Many engineers who study in one field can still work in other engineering fields, study a new field, and work in industries such as the fashion and music industries!

** Civil Engineering **

Civil engineers plan, design, and supervise the construction and maintenance of building and infrastructure projects. (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics)

** Electrical Engineering **

Electrical engineers design, develop, test, and supervise the manufacture of electrical equipment. (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics)

** Mechanical Engineering **

Mechanical engineers design, develop, build, and test mechanical and thermal sensors and devices. (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics)

** Computer Engineering **

At its core, a computer engineer is a professional who combines expertise in computer science and electrical engineering to design, develop, and maintain computer systems and networks. (Field Engineer)

** Chemical Engineering **

Chemical engineers apply the principles of chemistry, physics, and engineering to design equipment and processes for manufacturing products such as gasoline, detergents, and paper. (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics)

** Industrial Engineering **

Industrial engineers devise efficient systems that integrate workers, machines, materials, information, and energy to make a product or provide a service. (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics)
